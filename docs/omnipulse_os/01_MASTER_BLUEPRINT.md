# Master Blueprint

## Executive Direction

We are building an AI-first global media operating system. The first customer is us. The second customer is the future SaaS market.

The product must let us create, govern, publish, measure, and optimize an expanding network of content channels. It should feel like a control room for a modern media company, not a loose pile of prompts, render scripts, and scheduling tools.

The central idea:

> One operating system that turns audience demand into original, compliant, platform-ready media at scale, then learns from every result.

This means the build must combine:

- trend intelligence,
- content strategy,
- AI generation,
- asset creation,
- video rendering,
- platform publishing,
- analytics,
- experiment management,
- compliance,
- cost accounting,
- monetization,
- and future SaaS billing.

## Mission

Build the most complete AI media machine we can responsibly build:

- It should create content faster than a traditional media team.
- It should be safer than ad hoc human posting.
- It should learn from data faster than manual creative teams.
- It should be modular enough to launch new channels and formats.
- It should be clean enough to become SaaS without rewriting the core.

## What "Take Over" Means Operationally

"Take over" does not mean spam, evasion, content theft, or blind mass automation. It means:

- take over the process with better systems,
- take over speed through repeatable workflows,
- take over quality through QA and evidence,
- take over distribution through platform-native packaging,
- take over learning through analytics loops,
- take over monetization through diversified revenue,
- and take over durability through compliance and account health.

The goal is dominance by compounding systems, not by shortcuts that platforms can punish.

## Product Definition

OmniPulse OS is a multi-channel, multi-platform AI content operating system with five permanent layers:

1. Intelligence
2. Creation
3. Distribution
4. Measurement
5. Optimization

Each layer must be independently testable and connected through a common data model.

## Core Jobs To Be Done

### For Our Internal Network

- Find what audiences are already reacting to.
- Decide which channel and pillar should respond.
- Generate original briefs with a clear hook, audience, promise, and format.
- Create scripts, visuals, voice, music, captions, overlays, and thumbnails.
- Render platform-specific versions.
- Check rights, originality, claims, policy, and brand risk.
- Schedule or publish to approved platforms.
- Measure results.
- Create variants from winners.
- Kill weak patterns.
- Report profit, cost, defects, and channel health.

### For Future SaaS Customers

- Create a workspace.
- Connect platform accounts.
- Choose a channel template or create a brand.
- Set policy risk tolerance and review rules.
- Generate and approve content.
- Publish within platform limits.
- Track analytics and ROI.
- Pay through PayPal subscription and usage credits.
- Invite team members.
- Export audit evidence when needed.

## Canonical Content Pillars

### 1. Provocative Fixes and Debate-Safe Tension

This pillar uses tension, contradiction, unpopular truths, quick corrections, myths, mistakes, and "why people get this wrong" framing.

Allowed:

- myths vs facts,
- bad advice corrected,
- surprising but supportable claims,
- debate prompts,
- product or habit comparisons,
- common mistakes,
- hot takes with evidence.

Disallowed:

- harassment,
- hate,
- protected-class targeting,
- medical, legal, or financial claims without review,
- misinformation,
- impersonation,
- panic content,
- misleading outrage.

Example channel families:

- HackThis
- WhyThough
- FixTheMyth
- RageFix
- BadAdviceLab

### 2. Visual Addiction and Satisfying Loops

This pillar focuses on visually rewarding loops, transformations, process videos, AI visual sequences, symmetrical edits, restorations, procedural animations, and calming/satisfying micro-stories.

Allowed:

- generated abstract loops,
- safe transformation sequences,
- before/after visuals,
- repair/restoration simulations when clearly original,
- relaxing visual edits,
- soft loops for background consumption.

Quality bar:

- no broken anatomy,
- no unreadable overlays,
- no visual artifacts that damage trust,
- no copied stock footage unless licensed and tracked.

Example channel families:

- OddlySatisfy
- ZenZone
- LoopLab
- SatisfyCore
- VisualPulse

### 3. Curiosity, Facts, Mysteries, and Explanations

This pillar uses curiosity gaps, surprising facts, mini-explainers, mysteries, history, science-adjacent stories, and "how it works" framing.

Allowed:

- fact explainers with sources,
- historical curiosities,
- mystery breakdowns,
- tech and science explanations,
- "did you know" formats,
- strange but verified phenomena.

Required:

- claims extraction,
- source links,
- confidence scoring,
- fact review for sensitive topics,
- correction workflow when wrong.

Example channel families:

- DidYouKnow
- FactQuest
- MindHook
- MysteryGrid
- WhatIfVerse

### 4. Comparisons, Rankings, and Versus Formats

This pillar turns choices into structured comparison: best vs worst, ranked lists, side-by-side differences, price/value comparisons, country/city comparisons, tools, habits, and products.

Allowed:

- ranked explainers,
- versus carousels,
- decision trees,
- poll-driven rankings,
- region comparisons with context,
- product comparisons with disclosures.

Required:

- criteria transparency,
- affiliate/sponsorship disclosure,
- avoidance of defamatory claims,
- source-backed factual comparisons.

Example channel families:

- Versus
- RankIt
- TopGrid
- BetterPick
- CompareLab

### 5. Practical Utility, Hacks, DIY, and Quick Fixes

This pillar provides short useful actions: household tips, software shortcuts, money-saving ideas, productivity flows, recipes, repairs, travel tips, and tool walkthroughs.

Allowed:

- safe hacks,
- clear step-by-step clips,
- checklists,
- shortcuts,
- templates,
- tool comparisons,
- daily-life improvements.

Required:

- safety checks,
- no dangerous home, health, legal, or financial advice without review,
- no false guarantee language.

Example channel families:

- QuickFix
- DIYMagic
- HackThis
- UtilityLab
- DailyShortcut

### 6. Emotional Stories, Human Moments, and Micro-Drama

This pillar uses short emotional arcs, fictionalized stories, relationship moments, redemption arcs, surprising endings, and empathy-driven narratives.

Allowed:

- fictional stories,
- clearly transformed anonymous stories,
- public-domain inspired narrative,
- motivational micro-drama,
- safe conflict and resolution.

Required:

- do not present fictional AI stories as real eyewitness reporting,
- avoid exploiting tragedy,
- no private-person defamation,
- no manipulated real-person identity.

Example channel families:

- HeartToHeart
- RealStories
- StoryRush
- MomentLab
- TinyDrama

### 7. World Polls, Social Experiments, and PulseWorld

This pillar turns global opinion into content. It includes polls, country-vs-country responses, "what would you choose" prompts, voting loops, rankings, and social comparison.

Allowed:

- platform-native polls,
- web-hosted polls,
- daily global questions,
- region-specific rankings,
- audience-generated choices,
- follow-up result videos.

Required:

- clear sampling disclaimers,
- bot and duplicate-vote filtering,
- region/language sensitivity,
- no false claims that poll results represent an entire country unless sampling supports it.

Example channel families:

- WorldSays
- VoteGlobal
- PulseWorld
- PollPlanet
- GlobalChoice

## Channel Factory

Channels are not hard-coded projects. They are records created from templates.

Each channel template must define:

- name,
- handle options,
- pillar,
- audience,
- language,
- region,
- tone,
- risk level,
- visual system,
- voice style,
- intro/outro rules,
- posting cadence,
- platforms,
- allowed formats,
- blocked topics,
- monetization routes,
- compliance gates,
- performance targets,
- retry and kill rules.

New channel creation should be possible through a wizard:

1. Choose pillar.
2. Choose target audience.
3. Choose language and region.
4. Choose tone and risk level.
5. Choose platforms.
6. Choose brand palette variation.
7. Generate channel kit.
8. Generate first 30 content briefs.
9. Run policy preflight.
10. Approve launch plan.

## Core Pipeline

### Stage 1: Signal Intake

Inputs:

- platform trends,
- keywords,
- competitor posts,
- comments,
- search demand,
- news/public web sources,
- evergreen topic banks,
- prior channel analytics,
- poll responses,
- seasonal calendar,
- affiliate product feeds,
- internal experiments.

Outputs:

- trend signal records,
- topic clusters,
- audience intent,
- freshness score,
- saturation score,
- risk score,
- estimated opportunity score.

### Stage 2: Strategy Selection

The system decides:

- which pillar should respond,
- which channel should use the idea,
- which format fits,
- whether it should be short-form, long-form, carousel, text, poll, or thread,
- whether it is evergreen or trend-sensitive,
- whether human review is required.

### Stage 3: Content Brief

Every generated piece starts with a brief:

- title candidate,
- hook,
- audience,
- platform,
- content promise,
- emotional driver,
- story arc,
- visual direction,
- claims to verify,
- sources needed,
- asset needs,
- risk notes,
- monetization angle,
- variant plan.

### Stage 4: Script and Storyboard

Outputs:

- script,
- shot list,
- scene timings,
- text overlays,
- caption plan,
- CTA,
- title options,
- description,
- hashtags,
- thumbnail concept,
- platform-specific copy.

Quality rule: scripts must be original and platform-native. No generic AI voice. No repetitive template sameness.

### Stage 5: Asset Creation

The system creates or selects:

- background video,
- generated stills,
- generated video clips,
- voiceover,
- music,
- sound effects,
- captions,
- overlays,
- icons,
- thumbnail assets,
- poll graphics.

Every asset must have:

- origin,
- provider,
- prompt or source reference,
- license/rights status,
- usage restrictions,
- content hash,
- associated project/post,
- approval status.

### Stage 6: Render

Render engine must produce:

- master file,
- platform aspect-ratio variants,
- captions burned or sidecar,
- thumbnail exports,
- safe-area checked text,
- audio loudness normalized,
- final metadata package,
- render manifest.

Target aspect ratios:

- 9:16 for Shorts, TikTok, Reels, Stories, Pinterest Idea-style formats where supported.
- 1:1 for square social.
- 16:9 for YouTube long-form and LinkedIn where appropriate.
- 4:5 for Instagram/Facebook feed when appropriate.

### Stage 7: Compliance Gates

Before publishing:

- rights check,
- originality check,
- duplicate check,
- platform policy check,
- fact check,
- AI label check,
- affiliate/sponsor disclosure check,
- sensitive-topic risk check,
- brand safety check,
- account health check,
- rate limit check,
- human review when required.

### Stage 8: Platform Packaging

One content item becomes multiple platform packages:

- title,
- caption,
- hashtags,
- description,
- thumbnail,
- video file,
- aspect ratio,
- privacy status,
- AI/sponsored/content disclosure flags,
- comment settings,
- audience settings,
- schedule time,
- account identity,
- retry policy.

### Stage 9: Publishing

Publishing should use official APIs when approved and available. Where APIs are restricted, the adapter must support:

- draft creation,
- manual-review queue,
- export package,
- webhook notification,
- checklist-based manual upload,
- later direct publishing when approved.

### Stage 10: Measurement

Measure:

- views,
- impressions,
- watch time,
- retention,
- average view duration,
- loop rate,
- CTR,
- shares,
- saves,
- comments,
- follows/subscribers,
- revenue,
- cost,
- policy issues,
- account health,
- publish success/failure,
- render defects.

### Stage 11: Optimization

The system should identify:

- winning hooks,
- weak openings,
- high-retention scenes,
- drop-off moments,
- best post times,
- high-performing topics,
- low-quality providers,
- high-cost/low-return formats,
- channels ready to scale,
- channels to pause.

### Stage 12: Variant and Remix

Allowed variants must be materially different:

- new opening,
- new script angle,
- new visual sequence,
- new narration,
- new platform-native format,
- new region/language framing,
- new poll result,
- new evidence/source update,
- compilation with added structure.

Disallowed:

- same video with only color swap,
- same script with tiny synonym changes,
- repeated upload across accounts to fake scale,
- AI-spun near-duplicates.

## Content Volume Strategy

The system should be able to scale, but scale is unlocked by gates.

Early target:

- 3 to 5 initial channel families,
- 2 to 3 platforms active,
- 5 to 20 generated items per day in internal draft,
- 1 to 5 published items per channel per day only after quality and account-health proof.

Growth target:

- 10 to 25 channel families,
- 5 to 8 platforms,
- multilingual variants,
- automated analytics,
- controlled variant generation,
- direct SaaS beta.

Enterprise target:

- hundreds of tenant channels,
- strict rate-limit scheduling,
- role-based approvals,
- tenant-specific compliance rules,
- usage-based billing,
- provider marketplace,
- template marketplace.

## Long-Form Documentary Engine

The newest context adds an important expansion: the system should not only create daily short-form content. It should also support weekly or monthly deep-dive documentaries.

The Documentary Engine is a premium production mode that turns one prompt into:

- a long-form documentary blueprint,
- multi-chapter outline,
- research dossier,
- source and claim ledger,
- chapter scripts,
- voiceover,
- stock/generative visual plan,
- chapter renders,
- final FFmpeg/Remotion assembly,
- YouTube long-form package,
- short-form cutdowns,
- cost and compliance report.

This should be built as multi-chapter orchestration, not as one giant generation call.

Default approach:

- 6-10 chapters,
- chapter-level research,
- chapter-level fact review,
- chapter-level render jobs,
- final assembly,
- derivative short-form clips.

The strategic value is strong:

- long-form builds authority,
- long-form creates watch-time depth,
- each documentary becomes many shorts,
- source research can be reused,
- premium sponsorship and affiliate opportunities are stronger,
- YouTube long-form becomes a natural anchor for the network.

This should not delay the short-form MVP. It should become a major phase after PolicyOS, rendering, YouTube packaging, analytics, and cost tracking are working.

The project succeeds when:

- We can launch a new channel from a template.
- We can generate original content with evidence and rights records.
- We can render platform-specific variants.
- We can publish or export safely.
- We can measure results automatically.
- We can improve future output based on analytics.
- We can avoid platform-risk behavior.
- We can support tenant separation and PayPal billing without architectural rewrite.
- We can prove every major workflow through QA/QC gates.

## Non-Negotiables

- Compliance is infrastructure, not an afterthought.
- Platform rules are connector configuration, not tribal knowledge.
- Every asset needs provenance.
- Every factual claim needs a source or a risk label.
- Every post needs a policy event trail.
- Every failure must become a measurable defect.
- Every provider cost must be tied to content output.
- Every channel must have a kill, pause, and scale rule.
- Every SaaS feature must preserve tenant separation.
- Every automation must be observable and reversible.
