# Channel and Content System

## Purpose

This document turns the source documents' channel concepts into a reusable channel factory. The names are not final locks. They are working inventory, strategy patterns, and templates we can test.

The system must be able to create:

- our owned channels,
- regional variants,
- language variants,
- SaaS customer channels,
- white-label channel templates,
- format-specific series,
- poll-driven properties,
- future niche properties.

## Channel Philosophy

A channel is not just a handle. It is an operating unit with:

- audience,
- promise,
- emotional engine,
- visual identity,
- content pillar,
- policy profile,
- platform plan,
- cadence,
- monetization route,
- analytics targets,
- scale rules,
- pause/kill rules.

Every channel should be measurable and repeatable.

## Canonical Pillars and Template Families

### Pillar 1: Provocative Fixes and Debate-Safe Tension

Purpose:

- convert tension into useful correction,
- create high-retention hooks,
- expose common mistakes,
- generate debate without unsafe manipulation.

Working channel names from source synthesis:

- HackThis
- WhyThough
- RageFix
- FixTheMyth
- BadAdviceLab
- TruthSwitch
- HotTakeLab
- StopDoingThis

Recommended formats:

- "Everyone says X. Here is what actually works."
- "Stop doing this mistake."
- "The advice that sounds smart but fails."
- "This common hack is not what you think."
- "Which side is right?"

Risk controls:

- source factual claims,
- avoid protected-class targeting,
- no harassment,
- no fake outrage,
- no medical/legal/financial/political claims without review.

Best platforms:

- TikTok,
- YouTube Shorts,
- Reels,
- X,
- Reddit only with manual community review.

Initial recommendation:

- Do not launch this pillar first at full force. Build it after PolicyOS proves itself on lower-risk pillars.

- PatternRoom

Recommended formats:

- color/material transition,

Risk controls:

## Recommended Launch Mix

The first channel wave should avoid overloading the riskiest content.

Recommended starting five:

1. FactQuest or MindHook
2. QuickFix or DailyShortcut
3. RankIt or CompareLab
4. LoopLab or SatisfyCore
5. PulseWorld

Hold until later:

- RageFix,
- HotTakeLab,
- high-conflict politics,
- medical claims,
- financial promises,
- relationship story formats presented as real.

## Scale, Pause, Kill Rules

### Scale Rule

Scale when:

- policy pass rate is stable,
- render defects are low,
- retention exceeds threshold,
- account health is clean,
- cost per qualified view is acceptable,
- at least one repeatable format shows consistent wins.

### Pause Rule

Pause when:

- account warning appears,
- policy block rate rises,
- duplicate score rises,
- audience negative feedback rises,
- provider cost spikes,
- render failures rise,
- publishing API failures repeat.

### Kill Rule

Kill or rebrand when:

- channel fails after multiple tested formats,
- audience fit remains weak,
- policy risk exceeds upside,
- monetization path is poor,
- format is too generic,
- production cost is structurally too high.
